What Makes Windscreens More Expensive?

ADAS Cameras and Sensors

Modern vehicles with lane-keep assist, autonomous emergency braking or adaptive cruise control have forward-facing cameras mounted to the windscreen. After replacement, these must be recalibrated so they read the road correctly. Recalibration costs $200 to $500 on top of the replacement.

Rain Sensors

Automatic wipers use a rain sensor attached to the glass. The replacement glass must have the correct sensor mounting, and the sensor must be reattached properly.

Heated Windscreens

Heated windscreens have fine wires embedded in the glass. Replacement glass must match this feature, adding $200 to $400.

Acoustic Glass

Luxury vehicles often have acoustic laminated glass for noise reduction. This premium glass costs significantly more than standard.

Does Insurance Cover Windscreen Replacement?

Most comprehensive car insurance policies in NZ include windscreen cover. Many insurers offer an excess-free windscreen add-on for around $30–$50 per year.

  • AA Insurance: No excess for repairs, $100 excess for replacement
  • State Insurance: No excess for chip repairs on some policies
  • AMI: Windscreen cover on comprehensive policies
  • Tower: Windscreen cover typically included

How to Save Money on Windscreen Replacement

  1. Repair chips immediately — A $60 chip repair prevents a $400 replacement
  2. Check your insurance first — You may be fully covered
  3. Get multiple quotes — Prices vary between providers
  4. Use a mobile service — Often competitive pricing with no workshop overhead

How Long Does Windscreen Replacement Take?

Most windscreen replacements take 1 to 2 hours. The urethane adhesive then needs time to cure before the vehicle is safe to drive. The safe drive-away time is typically 1 to 3 hours depending on the adhesive type and weather conditions. We will tell you the exact safe drive-away time before we leave.
